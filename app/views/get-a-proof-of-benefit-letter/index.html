Change log

Since version 2, we have:

  • corrected benefit names to include acronyms on the start page
  • added signposting content for people who get benefits on behalf of a child, and people who live in Wales on the start page
  • made radio content on ‘how did you find out about this service?’ page more specific
  • included names of other services that use OIDV to ‘we are taking you to our identity checking service’ page to help registered users understand whether they have an account or not
  • merged ‘which benefits do you need proof of?’ and ‘getting proof of your benefits’ pages for users who get combination of in-and-out-of-scope benefit pages - checkbox version for multiple in-scope benefits and a radio version for one in-scope benefit

  • iterated ‘check your answers’ designs so that users select ‘change’ for the request, rather than just the benefit,
  • designed a ‘check your answers’ page for a person with one benefit

Version 1

! Warning The dynamic content in this design is for user testing only

We’re conducting A/B testing to see which flow works best for users.

We tested this design in user testing round 20.

Flow A: account homepage

Flow A has an account homepage with two links on: view your personal details, and get your proof of benefit letter.

Flow B: personal details homepage

Flow B has a homepage with personal details on, and a link to the proof of benefits feature.

Change log

This version includes:

  • A happy path for users who get in-scope benefits
  • A happy path for users whose address is correct
  • Signposting for users whose benefits are out-of-scope
  • Signposting for users whose address needs updating
  • Dynamic content depending on the benefits the user gets
  • Dynamic content depending on whether the user has a correspondence address saved on our records
  • The OIDV journey
