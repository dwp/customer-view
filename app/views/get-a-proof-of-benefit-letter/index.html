Feb 2025 - March 2025

Version 6: release 2 - PIP and NI

Release 2 is an exact replica of iteration 5A, with the addition of State Pension signposting.

We have also included the Northern Ireland design, which signposts users in NI to nidrect instead of GOV.UK. The changes impact the URLs and the hyperlink content. NI will use their own start page on nidirect and not use ours on GOV.UK.

Change log

New dynamic State Pension content included:

  • in the ‘tell us about a change’ section on the personal details page
  • when a user’s address is incorrect, we signpost them to SP change of circs on the address signpost page

Links that have been updated with NI content:

  • beta signposting pages when people have not been invited to service
  • benefits in and out of scope pages when a user is in the service and their benefits are not in scope
  • if the user’s address is incorrect, they get signposted to nidirect

Since release 1:

  • iterated the service name to ‘Get proof of your benefits’ so there are options to include more ways to get letters in future
  • corrected benefit names to include acronyms on the start page
  • added signposting content for people who get benefits on behalf of a child, and people who live in Wales on the start page
  • made radio content on ‘how did you find out about this service?’ page more specific
  • merged ‘which benefits do you need proof of?’ and ‘getting proof of your benefits’ pages for users who get combination of in-and-out-of-scope benefit pages - checkbox version for multiple in-scope benefits and a radio version for one in-scope benefit
  • iterated H1 content on some signposting pages to ‘contact us’ message rather than ‘you cannot use this service’
  • iterated ‘check your answers’ designs so that users select ‘change’ for the request, rather than just the benefit
  • designed a ‘check your answers’ page for a person with one benefit
  • corrected phase banner so that content matches guidelines
  • made headings on personal details table plural if user receives multiple benefits
  • Changed how we refer to getting proof of benefits across the journey - with the exception of the service name, removing ‘letter’ in many cases since users are getting an email
  • added signposting for users who get one in scope benefit and any amount of out of scope benefits, when they select ‘no’ - these are unhappy paths following the main changes in iteration 3
  • Iterated confirmation page to include what happens next for users who are getting proof in an email

Version 5 - PiP

! Warning The dynamic content in this design is for user testing only

This design is a direct copy of iteration 4, minus the option to get an email, and plus Personal Independence Payment (PIP).

This iteration will be tested in...

Change log

Since version 4, we have:

  • iterated the service name to ‘Get proof of your benefits’ so there are options to include more ways to get letters in future
  • deleted any reference to emails used in the last version while we assess how we’ll give people digital proof of their benefits
  • included content to let users know PIP is part of the service - on the start page in the bullet points and on the page when users select which benefit they want proof of
  • removed details on the page before OIDV that tells users who have registered for OIDV what other services they may have used to register

We’ve created two journeys to test with users:

Version A of the designs includes the PIP changes mentioned above to lets users know they can use the service if they get PIP. It doesn’t have any extra information about PIP.

Version B includes more details:

  • added signposting on the start page for people who want a PIP letter that includes points
  • added information that explains what’s included in the letter and signposting for people who want a letter that has points
  • Iterated content on the incorrect address signposting page
