Get a proof of benefit letter

Use this service to get proof of your:

  • Attendance Allowance
  • Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for adults
  • any type of Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Pension Credit
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • State Pension

It can take up to one week for your letter to arrive in the post.

If you need proof of your PIP points

To get a letter that includes points from your PIP assessment, contact PIP (opens in a new tab).

When to contact us

You cannot use this service if you:

  • need proof of a different benefit
  • are an appointee or manage somebody else’s benefits
  • have changed address and need to tell us your new one
  • need a letter in Welsh or an alternative format, like braille
  • do not live in England or Scotland

Find the contact details for the benefit that pays you in a letter they’ve sent to you, or in guidance about the benefit on GOV.UK (opens in a new tab).

If you need proof of Universal Credit, sign in to your Universal Credit account.

Before you start

You’ll need to register if you have not used this service before.

To register, you’ll need:

  • an email address
  • a mobile phone
  • your National Insurance number

To keep your information secure, we will ask you security questions to prove your identity.

If you have already registered

You’ll need the email address, mobile phone and password you used to register.
