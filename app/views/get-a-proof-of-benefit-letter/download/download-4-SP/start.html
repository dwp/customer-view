Get proof of your benefits and State Pension

Use this service to get proof of:

  • Attendance Allowance
  • Disability Living Allowance (DLA) for adults
  • any type of Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)
  • Income Support
  • Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA)
  • Pension Credit
  • Personal Independence Payment (PIP)
  • State Pension

You can view and save proof of your benefits or State Pension to your device.

You can also get a letter sent by post, which takes up to one week to arrive.

When to contact us

You cannot use this service if you:

  • need proof of a different benefit
  • are an appointee or manage somebody else’s benefits or State Pension
  • have changed address and need to tell us your new one
  • need a letter in an alternative format, like braille
  • do not live in England, Scotland, Wales or Northern Ireland

To get proof of your benefits or State Pension another way, like on the phone, contact each one that pays you.

Find the contact details for your benefit in a letter they’ve sent to you, or in the benefit guidance on GOV.UK (opens in a new tab).

For State Pension, contact the Pension Service (opens in a new tab).

If you need proof of Universal Credit, sign in to your Universal Credit account.

Before you start

You’ll need to register if you have not used this service before.

To register, you’ll need:

  • an email address
  • a mobile phone
  • your National Insurance number

To keep your information secure, we will ask you security questions to prove your identity.

If you have already registered

You’ll need the email address, mobile phone and password you used to register.
